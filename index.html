Mark E. Fuller, Ph.D.

Research Engineer

I am currently a member of the Physico-Chemical Fundamentals of Combustion research group at RWTH Aachen University. For a high-level overview, I have profiles in the usual places: LinkedIn, Google Scholar, ORCID, &c...

My current and past research interests include:

  • Experimental and theoretical examination of the role of nitrogenated compounds in combustion processes
  • Air-independent propulsion including batteries, fuel cells, and monopropellants
  • Emissions measurement and modelling
  • Heat transfer, particularly in nanofluids

My broader research goals are:

  1. Development of sustainable and renewable energy solutions
  2. Harm reduction in energy generation both for human health and environmental effects
  3. Promote and contribute open-source and open-access data and resources (contributions on github and gitlab)

External resources and links:

  • MIT's Reaction Mechanism Generator (github)
  • The Cantera suite of chemistry codes (github)
  • NumFocus promotes open data, research, and computing
